Help & Tips

Your recovery toolkit

Frequently Asked Questions

Use the 5 D's strategy:

  • Delay: Wait 10 minutes - most cravings pass
  • Distract: Do something else to occupy your mind
  • Deep breathe: Use our breathing exercise tool
  • Drink water: Stay hydrated and occupy your hands
  • Discuss: Talk to someone or use CalmBot

Common triggers include:

  • Stress: Work pressure, deadlines, conflicts
  • Social situations: Being around others who use substances
  • Emotions: Sadness, anger, boredom, or even happiness
  • Habits: After meals, during breaks, morning routine
  • Environmental cues: Certain places or times of day

Track your triggers in the craving log to identify patterns.

Remember: A slip is not a failure.

Relapse is often part of the recovery journey. What matters is what you do next:

  1. Be kind to yourself - shame doesn't help
  2. Identify what triggered the relapse
  3. Learn from the experience
  4. Recommit to your goals
  5. Reach out for support

Your progress isn't erased. Each attempt teaches you something valuable.

Our breathing exercise uses box breathing (4-4-4-4), a technique used by Navy SEALs and therapists:

  • Breathe in for 4 seconds
  • Hold for 4 seconds
  • Breathe out for 4 seconds
  • Hold for 4 seconds

This activates your parasympathetic nervous system, reducing anxiety and cravings.
